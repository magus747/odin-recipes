Palak Paneer

Description

This dish is a recurring weekday favorite at our house. It is easy to prepare, high in protein, and, most importantly, very tasty! The spice amounts are approximate. I usually add them to taste. For example, if you don't want a very strong stew, you can be careful with the chili or, if you love heat, just go ahead and add a lot! Experiment and find a mix that suits you.

Ingredients

  • 2 packages paneer cheese (about 450 g)
  • 200 g fresh spinach
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 piece of ginger (about 2-3 cm)
  • 1 tsp coriander
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 5 dl water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 pinch black pepper
  • Lemon juice

Steps

  1. Start by boiling the water and chopping the onion, garlic, and ginger.
  2. In a large pot, sauté the spices in butter for a few minutes (don't skimp on the butter!).
  3. Add the onion and garlic and sauté for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  4. Stir in the flour, and then gradually pour in the hot water while whisking to thicken the sauce.
  5. Add the spinach and let the dish simmer for a few minutes until the spinach wilts and the sauce thickens.
  6. Use a hand blender to quickly blend the sauce (if you prefer whole spinach leaves, you can skip this step).
  7. Cut the paneer cheese into small cubes (about 1x2 cm) and fry in a frying pan until lightly browned.
  8. Add the paneer to the sauce and season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice.
  9. Serve with freshly cooked rice.
